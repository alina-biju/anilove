What Are We?

From an angora cat to a yellow duckling, we've encountered stray or abandoned animals. We've even seen a baby bunny with a ribbon around its neck that was clearly an Easter gift. We were appalled to see such such treatment of animals. Many were thin, filthy, clearly hungry, andÂ injured. We couldn't just stand there or go by and do nothing. But what are we going to do? We had no idea. So we devised a plan in which we could all contribute.
Cats and dogs are the most commonly encountered strays. People adopt them as babies and are then burdened with vet and grocery expenditures. That is usually when the adult version is turned upside down in favor of another cute and cuddly small one. The creatures, who were fed and kept warm and cozy during their infancy, have no idea how to survive outside. They are unable to hunt and are unable to battle for refuge. They are essentially abandoned to perish.
And so, 'Anilove' was born.

Our Idea

Consider yourself travelling through your neighbourhood on a bright sunny morning and coming across a stray cat or dog that has been severely injured. What would your reaction be? So we created a platform where, if you come across a stray animal, you may notify pet agencies by using the 'Upload' feature on our website. The agency nearest to your area will be able to attend to the animal once they receive your message. This way, we can ensure that these creatures receive the care they require before it is too late. As responsible residents of this world, it is our responsibility to look after one another, exactly as Sustainable Development Goal 15 - 'Life on Land' proposes. We identified this goal, and our idea contributes to it. We can all help to bring about the transformation that the planet requires. Will you be a part of this change? Still, want to help these creatures? Go to the 'Adopt a Pet' page, where you can give a friendly creature a home. You may contact these agencies for further information by visiting their website.
